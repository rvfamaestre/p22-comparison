\subsection{A Short Description}
This project studies mixed traffic on a ring road where human-driven vehicles (HDVs) and connected autonomous vehicles (CAVs) share the same lane. The objective is to reduce traffic-wave amplification without giving up the robustness of a standard longitudinal controller.

Our approach keeps a microscopic car-following controller at vehicle level and adds a mesoscopic adaptation layer that reacts to upstream traffic conditions. Reinforcement learning is used only as a residual correction around a rule-based baseline, so the controller remains interpretable while still being able to adapt to traffic patterns that are difficult to encode by hand.
